## [12 Papers Built All of Modern AI. Here's How.](https://youtu.be/GyS5u20byRE?si=OYV-lb4eIOcaAOmT)

### Description Summary
This video by Claudius Papirus provides a historical and technical roadmap of the foundational research papers that transformed AI from a niche field into an era-defining technology. It traces the "chain reaction" of breakthroughs—from the invention of the Transformer in 2017 to the open-source release of LLaMA in 2023—explaining how each layer of the "AI stack" was built.

### TL;DR
- **Modern AI is a "Stack":** Current breakthroughs are not single inventions but a cumulative series of research milestones, starting from basic neural network training to sophisticated attention mechanisms.
- **The Transformer is the Core:** The "Attention Is All You Need" (2017) paper is identified as the most critical pivot, replacing old architectures with the "Self-Attention" mechanism that powers all modern LLMs.
- **Scaling Laws and Emergence:** Simply scaling up models (more data, more compute) leads to "emergent" abilities like coding and reasoning that weren't explicitly programmed.
- **Architecture over Coding:** For developers, understanding system architecture—how data, safety, and models fit together—is becoming more important than just writing code.
- **Data as a Garden:** Success in AI (80%) comes from "unsexy" work like data cleaning and constant "tending" to prevent data drift as the world changes.

### Summary
The video explores twelve foundational papers that serve as the backbone of modern AI. It begins by establishing the "pre-history" of AI with backpropagation and convolutional neural networks, then moves into the "Deep Learning Revolution" triggered by AlexNet and word embeddings like Word2Vec. The narrative focuses heavily on the "Transformer Era," explaining how the concept of "Attention" allowed models to process information in parallel and understand context far better than previous sequential models. It further discusses the evolution from raw base models to instruction-following assistants via Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) and the move towards open-source efficiency with models like LLaMA. The overarching message is that we are transitioning from simple chatbots to autonomous AI agents, where the focus shifts from dialogue to execution and system-wide architectural integrity.

### Links Referred in the Video and Description
- [Attention Is All You Need (Vaswani et al., 2017)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762) - The foundational paper for the Transformer architecture.
- [BERT: Pre-training of Deep Bidirectional Transformers (Devlin et al., 2018)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.04805) - Pre-training on massive data for task-agnostic bases.
- [GPT-2: Language Models are Unsupervised Multitask Learners (Radford et al., 2019)](https://cdn.openai.com/better-language-models/language_models_are_unsupervised_multitask_learners.pdf) - Early GPT scaling and zero-shot capabilities.
- [Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models (Kaplan et al., 2020)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.08361) - Empirical laws for model performance relative to compute and data.
- [GPT-3: Language Models are Few-Shot Learners (Brown et al., 2020)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.14165) - Emergent abilities through massive scaling.

- [Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models (Ho et al., 2020)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.11239) - Foundational math for image generation like Midjourney.
- [InstructGPT: Training language models to follow instructions (Ouyang et al., 2022)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.02155) - Introduction of RLHF for alignment.
- [Chinchilla: Training Compute-Optimal Large Language Models (Hoffmann et al., 2022)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.15556) - Insights on data-optimal scaling.

- [Constitutional AI (Bai et al., 2022)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.08073) - Self-alignment for AI safety.
- [LLaMA: Open and Efficient Foundation Language Models (Touvron et al., 2023)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.13971) - High-performance open-source foundation models.
